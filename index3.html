Implementation of Machine learning for voltage generation from a piezomaterial in a fluid flow

Abstract

In this study, the supervised machine learning techniques were employed to develop a model based on experimental data obtained from a water tunnel designed for harnessing energy through piezoelectric materials. The investigation involved the determination of optimal voltages for different input parameters, including distance and angle. Two regression methods, namely linear and polynomial regressions, were utilized for this purpose. The outcomes of the analysis revealed that polynomial regression exhibited a relative error of less than 6% when compared to linear regression. Subsequently, the model's accuracy was verified through validation using independent test data. Specifically, for a set of defined inputs (distance: 50 cm, depth: 5.1 cm, angle: 35 degrees), the optimal voltages were identified as 315.62 volts for the linear regression model and 218.64 volts for the polynomial regression model. Moreover, the study extended its scope by considering additional inputs for a fixed distance of 70 cm. In this scenario, the optimal voltage was determined using linear regression, focusing on a fixed depth of 4 cm.

Details

In the course of this investigation, our focus was on the application of supervised machine learning techniques to construct a model based on experimental data derived from a water tunnel specifically designed for harnessing energy through piezoelectric materials. The crux of the study lay in discerning optimal voltages for various input parameters, such as distance and angle. Two regression methods, specifically linear and polynomial regressions, played a pivotal role in shaping this analytical journey. Notably, the outcomes of our analysis unveiled the superiority of polynomial regression.
