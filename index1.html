Advanced Animal Health Monitoring Through Posture Analysis

Abstract

In this study, state-of-the-art technologies, including deep learning, image processing, and signal processing, have been leveraged to address the pressing concern of lameness in cattle. Lameness is a significant issue with far-reaching consequences, affecting both the well-being of the animals and the overall profitability of farms [1-2]. To tackle this problem, the research focuses on applying advanced methods such as posture estimation and the analysis of gait cycles in both lame and healthy cows. These technologies play a crucial role in identifying and comprehending early indicators of lameness. By closely examining the posture and gait patterns of cows, the study aims to establish a comprehensive understanding of the factors contributing to lameness. The ultimate goal is to develop a reliable system for early detection of lameness. This system would facilitate continuous health monitoring, allowing farmers to identify signs of lameness at its onset. By detecting lameness in its early stages, farmers can implement timely interventions to mitigate its impact on individual animals and the overall herd. This proactive approach not only improves the welfare of the animals but also contributes to the enhanced efficiency and profitability of the farm [3-7].

Details

At the 24th Annual Alberta Biomedical Engineering Conference held in Banff, AB, on October 27-28, 2023, I presented my research a poster session. The focus of the study revolves around addressing the critical issue of lameness in cattle, utilizing state-of-the-art technologies such as deep learning, image processing, and signal processing. In the presented research, advanced methods such as posture estimation and gait cycle analysis were employed to delve into the complexities of lameness in both healthy and lame cows. The significance of this research lies in its potential to identify early indicators of lameness by closely examining the posture and gait patterns of the animals. The overarching goal is to develop a reliable system for the early detection of lameness, enabling continuous health monitoring on farms.
