Animal Gait Cycle Signals: Transforming Eulerian to Lagrangian coordinates

Abstract

The extraction of movement signals from 2D images of animals poses challenges, primarily arising from the animal's non-linear movements in various directions and potential interference caused by the camera moving as the animal walks. This study presents a novel methodology for extracting signalsfrom a cow’s gait cycle to address the existing challenges in the 2D video. The innovation involves a transformation of images from Eularian or global coordinates to Lagrangian or local coordinates, with establishing a consistent reference point on the cow's body via region-based centroid calculation. By employing image segmentation, image processing, 2D cross-correlation, and a centroid-based approach, reliable reference points are extracted on the cow body, resulting in the signal extraction in the Lagrangian coordinate system from anatomical keypoints. This coordinate system transformation enables the exclusion of motion- influenced segments to identify a fixed region on the cow’s body. To validate the signal extraction process, the signals of video frames and the signals of analytical method are compared using a simulated sinusoidal signal in the Lagrangian coordinate system. It was demonstrated that the applied transformation procedure has 99.83% accuracy. This study contributes valuable insights into animal movement analysis, presenting an innovative signal extraction methodology with implications for signal processing in animal science studies.
